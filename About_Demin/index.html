Education and Work

Education

I have my bachelors degree from Middle Tennessee State University in psychology with a double minor in history and women and gender studies. I was involved in Muslim Inter-Scholastic Tournament (MIST) as a competitor in high school and as a board member in college. I was also the treasurer of the history club and the secretary of Phi Alpha Theta Honors Society in college. I also received a fulbright scholarship to relocate to Malaysia as an English Teaching Assistant (ETA) and cultural ambassador for 11 months. Unfortunately, I was unable to go due to COVID.

Work

My first job was at Tj Maxx as a sales consultant while I was in high school. Then I transitioned to a server position at Carrabbas to help pay for college, and I worked there full time until I graduated. After graduation, I began working as a Family Service Worker at the Department of Children's Services to help foster youth reunite with their biological families. My most recent position is Foster Parent Support with the Department of Children's Services where I support foster parents and help the department with recruitment.
